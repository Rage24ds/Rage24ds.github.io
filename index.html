PETROLEUM

Petroleum is a resource that is extracted from the ground, and is turned into many different fuel sources, the fuel sources that is is turned into is petrol, gasoline, diesel, propane, jet fuel, heating oil, and paraffin wax, the way that it is made is that organic matter settles in water, and loses all its oxygen, then is compressed under a large amount of heat and pressure for millions of years, once all of this has happened you get petroleum.

NATURAL GAS

Natural gas this fuel is a byproduct of organic matter decomposing, and is mostly methane, once the Natural gas has been extracted it is then processed to remove everything from the gas but the methane, the gasses produces many other fuels such as ethane, propane, and butane all if these products are used as fuel to heat home businesses and fuel stoves, it is fairly clean burning but like the rest it is not renewable.

COAL

Coal is composed organic matter, it is matter that decomposed in peat bogs, and then under a lot of pressure it was formed into a carbon rock, coal is highly combustible and is the world's most used resource for electric production, but coal when burned releases massive amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, and is the primary factor for the greenhouse effect, so if we were to stop using it after a couple decades the greenhouse effect would begin to retreat, after a couple million years greenhouse gasses will be at normal levels.
